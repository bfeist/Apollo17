Instructions:

  • Chrome / Firefox / Safari preferred. Works on iPad with patience.
  • Click transcript items and playback will jump to that moment.
  • For big jumps, use the mission navigator at the top of the page (or table of contents tab)
  • Jump to different points using the video scrubber and playback will jump to that moment.

Apollo 17 in Real-Time

A real-time interactive exploration of Apollo 17 - The Last Mission to the Moon.

Watch and listen to every moment of the mission--over 300 hours of audio transmitted, 550,000+ words spoken and transcribed, 4,200+ photos taken, and 22+ hours of television transmitted from the lunar surface. All media is coordinated within the mission's timeline. Video, photography, and every word spoken is played back in real-time, as the events occurred back in 1972.

Below you can choose to start the mission at 1 minute to launch, or join it in-progress as occurred at today's date and time, 43 years ago.

"When I left the Moon and started up the ladder, I was really at a loss. I didn’t want to leave and I looked down at my last footsteps and realised I wasn’t coming this way again. Looking back over my shoulder at the Earth had a particular significance to me – it was alive, it was moving, with purpose and beauty through space and time. In those short few minutes I wanted to figure out what was the meaning of us – everyone alive in the world today – leaving the cradle of civilization and calling the Moon our home for a few days. I searched for that answer, I needed more time. I wanted to press the freeze button, stop time to give myself a chance to think about it.
- Gene Cernan, Commander, Apollo 17. The last man to walk on the moon.

Mission Navigator

This tool allows you to navigate to any time within the mission.

The top bar represents the entire mission. (13 days wide)
The 2nd bar is a zoomed view of part of the top bar. (~15 hours wide)
The 3rd bar is a zoomed view of part of the 2nd bar. (~1.5 hours wide)
Grey: mission event
Green: photograph taken
Blue: Words spoken by the crew or mission control
Purple: Mission Video
